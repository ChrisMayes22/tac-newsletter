What an exciting year 2018 has been for our students! While the calendar year is winding down and Winter Break is fast approaching, students at TAC are still working hard with important benchmarks on the horizon. For younger students, this means working on organizational skills and core subject areas that will be vital for success in later grades, as well as preparing for ISEE and SSAT for those who wish to attend prep school. For high schoolers, it means focusing both on academic coaching for tough AP courses, which prepare for the rigor of college, and on the ACT and SAT. And for college students, it means preparing for the

self-teaching necessary to thrive in an increasingly tough university environment in addition to LSAT, GRE, and GMAT prep for those whose goals involve graduate school. For high schoolers, determining the optimal testing timeline for the ACT/SAT, is one of one of our most important objectives. All too often students (and their parents) are advised to delay testing until the later stages of Junior year. This standard but outdated recommendation only makes sense for a small segment of students who are struggling to keep up academically. In reality, most students are primed to achieve desired ACT/SAT test scores Sophomore year.
